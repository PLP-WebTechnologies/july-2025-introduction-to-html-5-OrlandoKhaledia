About This Page

Rwanda is a landlocked country in East-Central Africa, bordered by Uganda, Tanzania, Burundi, and the Democratic Republic of Congo.
Known as the "Land of a Thousand Hills," Rwanda is famous for its lush green landscapes, rolling hills, and biodiversity.
The capital city, Kigali, is considered one of the cleanest and safest cities in Africa. Rwanda has four official languages: Kinyarwanda, English, French, and Swahili.

Culture & Society

Rwanda is known for its strong community values, cultural unity, and emphasis on reconciliation after the 1994 genocide.
The country places a high value on education, gender equality, and public service.
Women hold over 60% of seats in the Rwandan parliament the highest percentage in the world.

Development & Economy

Over the past two decades, Rwanda has made remarkable strides in economic development, digital transformation, and infrastructure.
It's considered a leader in green energy, ICT innovation, and business reform in Africa.

Did You Know?

People & Culture Rwandans value community, family, and respect. Music, dance, and storytelling are part of traditional life.
Despite its tragic history in 1994, Rwanda is now a global example of unity and reconciliation.
